Carina


is a database of RNA post-transcriptional regulators and event associated alternative splicing with health and disease of the cardiovascular system.

doRiNA and its web-interface, webDorina, focused on to collect the interactions between post-transcriptional regulators, specifically microRNAs (miRNAs) and RNA-binding proteins (RBPs). Carina extends this functionally by:

  • including recent experiments that identify RBPs and its targets
  • integration genomic variation that may be relevant to the heart malfunction
  • facilitating the filtering for genes that are expressed or enriched in heart disease
  • curating the elements relevant to heart physiology
  • providing new ways to integrate and visualize the above listed

The tool is developed by Thiago Britto-Borges and Christoph Dieterich from the Dieterich Lab Section of Bioinformatics and Systems Cardiology, Klaus Tschira Institute for Integrative Computational Cardiology Department of Internal Medicine III - University Hospital Heidelberg and German Center for Cardiovascular Research (DZHK) - Partner site Heidelberg/Mannheim.
