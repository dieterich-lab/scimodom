RMaP


is a database (under construction) of RNA modifications that allows to perform queries to determine if a site is subject to modification or processing events.

RMap aims to address the following:

  • harmonize transcriptome-wide data exchange formats and interoperability standards
  • provide a critical assessment of and integration with public data
  • encompass all known RNA modifications across multiple assays or experimental techniques
  • integrate data from different model organisms and/or tissues.

RMaP uses the doRiNA code base and its web-interface is currently build on the webDorina application. RMap is developed by the Dieterich Lab , Section of Bioinformatics and Systems Cardiology, Klaus Tschira Institute for Integrative Computational Cardiology Department of Internal Medicine III - University Hospital Heidelberg and German Center for Cardiovascular Research (DZHK) - Partner site Heidelberg/Mannheim.
